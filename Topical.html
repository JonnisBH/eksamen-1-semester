If you use social media, log into one of your accounts and have a look at the advertisements. Can you trace them back to web pages you have visited or posts you have interacted with?

The first thing the group did in this task was to log in to one of our Instagram accounts to see if any ads appeared about something we have a relation to. That did indeed happen and after a while advertisement for coding appeared. This is interesting considering that in recent weeks, coding related websites have been used in frequent sequences for school. After further investigations through the advertising, we arrived at the website w3schools. This is a website that is mainly about coding, and is in fact a page all of us in the group have used before. After this revelation, we were curious to see if it was deliberate enough for it to affect the other accounts, and so we moved on to the next person in the group's Instagram to see which ads appeared there. We came across several sources that were football and fantasy football related, and when we went into more of the details we saw several sites that were familiar to us. These were pages that have been used in the past by one or more in the group. The most reoccuring website from the ads is called Fantasy Premier League and is a page about fantasy football.

We noticed that after we started studying IT, we received significant amounts of recommended code videos on Tiktok. This appears regardless of whether we have searched for coding in Tiktok's search field. It is also experienced at times where someone has talked about traveling abroad and then the advertisement recommends Trivago and other airlines afterwards. It creates a theory that the phone is actively using the microphone to adapt ads, which is unexpected.
